Follow these exact steps to activate the Google Login button:

## 1. Create a Project
1. Open the [Google Cloud Console](https://console.cloud.google.com/).
2. Sign in with your Google Account.
3. Click the dropdown menu at the top-left (next to the Google Cloud logo).
4. Click **"New Project"** at the top right of the popup.
5. Project Name: **Kismath Web** (or anything you like).
6. Click **Create**.
7. Wait a moment, then click **"Select Project"** in the notification bell, or select it from the top dropdown.

## 2. Configure Consent Screen
1. In the left sidebar, go to **APIs & Services** > **OAuth consent screen**.
2. Select **External** (User Type).
3. Click **Create**.
4. **App Information**:
   - **App name**: Kismath
   - **User support email**: Select your email.
   - **Developer contact information**: Enter your email again.
5. Scroll down and click **Save and Continue**.
6. **Scopes**: Just click **Save and Continue** (skip this).
7. **Test Users**: Just click **Save and Continue** (skip this).
8. Click **Back to Dashboard** on the Summary page.

## 3. Create Credentials (The ID)
1. In the left sidebar, click **Credentials**.
2. Click **+ CREATE CREDENTIALS** (at the top).
3. Select **OAuth client ID**.
4. **Application type**: Choose **Web application**.
5. **Name**: Leave as "Web client 1" or name it "Kismath Login".
6. **Authorized JavaScript origins**:
   * Click **+ ADD URI**.
   * Type: `http://localhost`
   * *(Optional: If you use a specific port like 8080, add `http://localhost:8080` as well)*.
7. Click **Create**.

## 4. Copy & Paste
1. A popup will appear with "Your Client ID".
2. **Copy** the long string ending in `.apps.googleusercontent.com`.
3. Open your project file: `login.php`.
4. Find line 89: `data-client_id="YOUR_GOOGLE_CLIENT_ID"`.
5. Replace `YOUR_GOOGLE_CLIENT_ID` with the code you just copied.

**Example:**
`data-client_id="9876543210-abcdefg12345.apps.googleusercontent.com"`

## 5. Test It
1. Restart your XAMPP Apache server (optional, but good practice).
2. Go to `http://localhost/kismath/login.php`.
3. You should see the Google button and be able to log in!
